General conditions for processing requests for comparability certificates for foreign diplomas

Introduction

You will submit a request for a certificate of comparability for your diploma obtained abroad or for a request for recognition of a period of study carried out abroad (from one year of post-secondary studies).

The ENIC-NARIC France center will process your request in two stages.

Careful!: you can only present one (1) diploma per certificate request.

Chapter I: Receivable files giving rise to recognition by the ENIC-NARIC France center

ARTICLE 1: Your file will be declared admissible if it is complete. Here is the list of documents to be provided.

ARTICLE 2: Your file will result in recognition by the ENIC-NARIC center if it is proven that your diploma is officially authorized/accredited/approved/recognized by the competent authorities of the country in which the diploma was issued.

Chapter II : Diplomas related to a regulated profession in France

ARTICLE 3 : Your application will be rejected in the first instance if the diploma in question is related to a regulated profession in France ( List of regulated professions in France ).

ARTICLE 4 : Your application will be rejected if the document concerns a diploma or certification in the medical and paramedical fields.

ARTICLE 5 : In the case of an application for a real estate business card, we can accept your application provided that proof of filing with the Chambre de commerce et d'industrie (CCI letterhead) is attached to the diplomas and documents submitted.
Please contact your local Chamber of Commerce and Industry to obtain this document.
ARTICLE 6 : EU and non-EU nationals wishing to become a teacher in a gym, sports educator, sports coach/personal fitness trainer, pilates instructor, etc. must imperatively go through the ARQUEDI application of the Ministry of Sports. You must submit a request for equivalence of your diploma obtained abroad by creating a file on the ARQUEDI application.
A few exceptions : if your diploma is related to a regulated profession in France for which an attestation of comparability can be issued under certain conditions, for example with a view to further study or in the case of practice as an employee ( regulated professional qualifications for which an attestation can be issued by the ENIC NARIC France center ).
As a holder of a diploma obtained in a European Union country, you wish to work as a indépendant in France, please consult this link and contact the Chambre des métiers et de l'artisanat (CMA) of your place of practice: Le portail des Chambres de Métiers et de l'Artisanat en France | Artisanat.fr
In any case, the certificate issued by ENIC-NARIC France should not be considered as an authorization to practice a regulated profession in France.

Chapter III : Documents not appraised by ENIC-NARIC France

ARTICLE 7 : Your application will be rejected if your diploma is a language certification (language diplomas or tests attesting to a level of language proficiency).

ARTICLE 8 : Your application will be rejected if the document submitted concerns non-diploma qualifying training and does not correspond to a precise academic level in the educational system of the country of origin.

ARTICLE 9 : The ENIC NARIC France center cannot process requests for military grades.

ARTICLE 10 : Your application will be rejected if the document submitted concerns a license to practice: teaching certificate, pastry chef title, beautician title, etc.


Chapter IV : Mention of course in French on certificate of comparability

ARTICLE 11 : In the case of an application for the exercise of a private security activity Decree no. 2022-209 of February 18, 2022 on the conditions governing the exercise of private security activities - Légifrance (legifrance.gouv.fr), you must prove that your diploma was awarded following a course of study in French. Your application will be accepted if you meet the following three conditions:
  • the country where you obtained your diploma is on the list of 31 states defined by Order of March 12, 2020 establishing the list of States provided for in a of 10° of Article 14-1 and a of 9° of Article 37-1 of Decree No. 93-1362 of December 30, 1993 as amended relating to declarations nationality, decisions on naturalization, reintegration, loss, forfeiture and withdrawal of French nationality - Légifrance (legifrance.gouv.fr) effective April 1, 2020;
  • You can present a certificate issued by the training institution certifying that the course has been followed mainly in French.
    Please note: bilingual courses are not accepted as part of this procedure.
  • Your diploma must be comparable to at least level 3 of the National Framework of Professional Certifications in France : https://www.enseignementsup-recherche.gouv.fr/fr/nomenclature-relative-au-niveau-de-diplome-45785
    Please note: this procedure only applies to attestations of comparability for foreign diplomas. Mention of the course in French is not possible for certificates of recognition of level of study.

    The modification of an attestation to subsequently add the mention of a course of study in French remains possible within the six (6) months following the date of issue of the attestation upon presentation of proof of completion of a course of study in French. After six (6) months, no modification is possible.
    Please note: Your application will be rejected if the document concerns a diploma or certification in the medical and paramedical fields, even for an application for naturalization/private security activity.


Chapter V : Processing times

ARTICLE 12 : The ENIC NARIC France Center processes files in principle in chronological order of arrival. The average processing time is 4 months, but can reach 6 months for some particularly complex cases.

In the case of an application submitted solely as part of a naturalization procedure, we draw your attention to other possible means of proving your level of French to the competent authorities, in particular the French knowledge test - integration, residence and nationality (TCF-IRN).

ARTICLE 13 : If your file is declared incomplete, you have one month to complete it. After this period, if you have not completed your file, it will no longer be accessible by our services, and you will have to submit a new file with the requested documents and pay the €20 administrative fee.

Please note: to make sure you don't miss any notifications from our platform, please check your inbox regularly and in particular your messages classified as spam.


Chapter VI : File processing and expert appraisal results

ARTICLE 14 : The receivability of a file in no way prejudges the favorable outcome of the expert appraisal. It cannot therefore be considered as an automatic guarantee that an attestation of comparability or an attestation of recognition of a period of study will be issued.

ARTICLE 15 : The attestation can be issued if the school where you studied and your diploma are officially recognized or accredited according to the procedures laid down in the education system of the country to which they belong. This information must be accessible and verifiable by our services before the ENIC-NARIC France center can issue a certificate. During the processing of your application, our experts may ask you for additional documents (transcripts, certificates of achievement, better scans).

ARTICLE 16 : As part of our expertise in foreign diplomas, our experts may need to request original documents to verify certain elements.

In this case, the applicant must send the original documents (diploma, certificate of achievement, transcript of grades, etc.) by a secure method (either registered post with acknowledgement of receipt, or Chronopost). The documents will then be returned - by registered mail with acknowledgement of receipt - to the applicant after expert examination, except in the case of documents which the competent authorities of the issuing country have confirmed to be falsified.

ENIC-NARIC France may need to verify the authenticity of documents submitted to the competent authorities in the country of issue. The following personal data may be transmitted: surname, first name, date and place of birth, copy of diploma, certificate of achievement and transcripts, in order to authenticate these documents. You may object to this transmission, but please note that in this case ENIC-NARIC France will not be able to issue a certificate of comparability.

In the case of refugees, asylum seekers and beneficiaries of subsidiary protection, ENIC-NARIC France will request prior written authorization from the user.

ARTICLE 17 : A certificate of comparability may be issued if the course of study on completion of which your diploma was awarded certifies a minimum of 200 hours of training and corresponds to a precise academic level in the country of origin.

ARTICLE 18 : In the case of periods of study, an attestation may be issued if the documents submitted attest to the validation of at least one (1) year of post-secondary studies.

ARTICLE 19 : A certificate can be issued if the diploma corresponds :
  • a minimum academic level at the end of the first cycle of secondary education (diplôme national du brevet)
  • or a level 3 of the national framework of professional certifications Cadre national des certifications professionnelles - Ministère du Travail, du Plein emploi et de l'Insertion (travail-emploi.gouv.fr)
Our center does not issue certificates of recognition for periods of secondary education.
Please contact the rectorat of the academy where the document holder wishes to continue his/her studies.


Chapter VII : Information provided on the certificate of comparability issued

ARTICLE 20 : On the basis of objective criteria (Grid of eligibility and assessment criteria), the expertise of the ENIC-NARIC France center may lead to the issue of a comparability certificate. The certificate will include the following details:
  • title of submitted document;
  • issuing establishment or organization;
  • official duration of studies;
  • academic and/or professional opportunities;
  • the result of an expert appraisal mentioning the level in the National Vocational Qualifications Framework and the European Qualifications Framework;
  • where applicable, a reference to the French-language curriculum of the diploma.

ARTICLE 21 : In the case of recognition of a period of study, the certificate will mention the following details:
  • title of submitted document;
  • training establishment;
  • number of years validated out of the number of years required to obtain the final diploma.
Chapter VIII : Personal data

France Éducation international carries out processing of personal data and undertakes to comply with all the provisions in force of Law No. 78-17 of January 6, 1978 relating to "information technology, files and freedoms" as amended and Regulation (EU) No. 2016/679 of the European Parliament and of the Council of April 27, 2016 known as the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).

Chapter IX : Claims and reimbursement

ARTICLE 22 : If you contest the decision rendered by the ENIC-NARIC France center, you are invited to send in writing a reasoned request, accompanied by all supporting documents to the following e-mail address: recours-enicnaric@france-educationinternational.fr
If you contest the rejection of your file for admissibility after the first payment of 20 €, your appeal must be sent to the above e-mail address within two (2) months of the notification from ENIC-NARIC France. No modification of the certificate can be made after this deadline.
If you dispute the result of expertise, for example concerning the level awarded, the duration of studies etc., you are invited to send in writing a reasoned request, accompanied by all supporting documents to the above e-mail address, and this within two (2) months of the date of issue of the attestation by the ENIC-NARIC France center. No modification of the attestation may be made after this deadline.
Appeals following an obvious error on the part of the ENIC-NARIC France center in the attestation, for example a spelling error in your name, an error in the title of the diploma, must be sent to the above e-mail address, within six (6) months following the date of issue of the attestation by the ENIC-NARIC France center. No modification of the attestation may be made after this deadline.

ARTICLE 23 : The modification of an attestation to subsequently add the mention of a course of study in French remains possible within the six (6) months following the date of issue of the attestation, and upon presentation of proof of completion of a course of study in French. After the six (6) month period, no changes are possible.

ARTICLE 24 : No refund will be made if the application is not accepted at the end of the first stage, or if an attestation of comparability or recognition of the period of study cannot be issued at the end of the second stage, or if the French curriculum cannot be specified.
